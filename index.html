Mathematical modeling of translation, degradation/secretion






  • at each level of the villus, we use mRNA to predict the protein levels at the next level
  • parameters:
    • $\beta$ : tells you how much translation is occurring
    • $\delta$ : tells you how much protein degradation/secretion is occurring
    • $t_{1/2}$ : tells you how unstable the protein is on its own

Bayesian model fitting allows for incorporation of literature knowledge





  • range of literature values used to force a range of likely values for the model

  • 4287 translation rates from literature

  • 5028 decay rates from literature

Model fits well, identifies different classes of concordant/discordant species

How about the intestine in general?

  • 40% of zonated genes (~55% of genes in study sample) have protein, mRNA anticorrelated
  • suggestive there is a division of labor from transcribing / translating machinery at bottom, to creating absorption machinery at top of villus
  • surprisingly respiration proteins high at the top (where there is no oxygen); also high levels of glutathione enzymes to protect against oxidation

Conclusions

  • enterocyte RNA/protein levels can be broken into discrete categories based on zone
  • global relationships across all transcripts/proteins not necessary to explain discordance

Future directions

  • would be great to follow up with specific exploration of what protein or transcript level characteristics influence translation or degradation rates
  • follow up on understanding what sorts of pathways are interacting intracellularly to create different cell states along the villus
